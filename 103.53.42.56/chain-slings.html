Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is a Chain Sling?

    • Slings are most commonly used in lifting gears that are applied in almost all industries where heavy load lifting is a requirement.

      Out of several types of slings available in the market, chain slings are the preferred ones when it comes to lifting very heavy loads. Therefore, **chain slings** for lifting are extensively used in shipping, rigging, aircraft and construction industry.

  • What is the relevance of chain grade?

    • The grades on a chain refer to the tensile strength of a particular chain and are expressed in newtons per square millimeter. Manufacturers use one-tenth of the actual grade and therefore, if the grade is 70 then it refers to 700, and so on.

  • Which grade of the chain is ideal and safe to be used in lifting slings?

    • Grade 80 chain will be a good choice for chain slings as it is a heat-treated steel chain and possesses excellent strength to weight ratio. This property makes such **chain slings** safe to use for lifting, towing or recovery.

  • Where are Chain Slings most commonly used?

    • Chain Slings are generally applied in the industrial and construction industry to lift loads as they are temperature resistant, sturdy and flexible, making them ideally suited for a wide array of loading conditions.
