Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Wire Rope?

    • A **Wire rope** is a mechanical device that is very commonly used in lifting and rigging industries, as it works to help support and move an object or load.

      A **wire rope** is attached to a crane or hoist and fitted with swivels, shackles or hooks in order to attach to a load and move it in a controlled manner.

  • What factors should be kept in mind while selecting a wire rope?

    • When selecting a **wire rope** there are 6 factors that should be kept in mind:


      1. Strength resistance to breaking

      2. Resistance to vibrational fatigue

      3. Resistance to abrasion

      4. Resistance to bending fatigue

      5. Resistance to crushing

      6. Reverse strength

  • What is the rope lay option?

    • All finished ropes have a left lay or a right lay, which indicates the direction in which the strands have been wrapped around.

      There are 2 lay options:


      1. Regular lay


      • Right Regular lay
      • Left Regular lay

      Lang’s lay


      • Right Lang lay
      • Left Lang lay
  • Why are wire ropes coated and what are the different types of wire rope coatings?

    • The need for coating the **wire ropes** is to provide the cable a protection layer from unwanted material or environment, to help minimize abrasion and friction, etc. The most commonly used coating material for **wire ropes** is PVC and zinc.
