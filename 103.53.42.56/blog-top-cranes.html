Top 10 Crane Manufacturers in India

A detailed and unbiased guide to India’s leading crane manufacturers delivering world-class EOT cranes, gantry cranes, and lifting solutions.

India’s rapid industrialization and infrastructure development have created a huge demand for reliable crane manufacturers. From overhead cranes and gantry cranes to specialized lifting solutions, both Indian and global companies have made significant contributions. Below, we explore the top 10 crane manufacturers in India, detailing their history, performance, strengths, and the unique qualities that justify their positions.

1. Mangla Hoists

Mangla Hoists, founded in 1973, has emerged as one of India’s most trusted names in the crane and hoist industry. With a legacy of more than five decades, the company specializes in wire rope hoists, EOT cranes, and custom material handling systems that cater to power plants, steel plants, warehouses, and infrastructure projects. Mangla Hoists has consistently focused on engineering innovation by introducing Industry 4.0-ready solutions such as IoT-based hoist monitoring, variable frequency drives (VFDs), and regenerative braking systems for energy efficiency. The company’s clientele includes industry giants like NTPC, BHEL, and L&T, highlighting its credibility in heavy-duty lifting solutions. With comprehensive after-sales support across India, Mangla Hoists has positioned itself as a leader that combines durability, modern technology, and reliability in material handling.

2. Konecranes India

Konecranes, headquartered in Finland, is a global leader in lifting equipment and has established a strong presence in India. The company is renowned for its digitalized and smart lifting solutions, offering advanced technologies such as TRUCONNECT® remote monitoring, anti-sway systems, and load control features. With decades of international experience, Konecranes combines its global R&D capabilities with solutions tailored to Indian industries, including automotive, logistics, and port operations. Its strong commitment to sustainability and eco-efficient designs further strengthen its position as a leading innovator in India’s crane market.

3. Action Construction Equipment (ACE)

Action Construction Equipment (ACE) was founded in 1995 and has grown into one of India’s largest manufacturers of material handling and construction equipment. ACE is particularly recognized for its dominance in mobile cranes and tower cranes, which are widely deployed in infrastructure projects such as highways, metro construction, and bridges. The company has built a reputation for delivering affordable, reliable, and performance-oriented cranes suited to India’s diverse construction needs. With a strong dealer and service network across the country, ACE remains the go-to brand for mobile crane solutions in India.

4. Larsen & Toubro (L&T)

Larsen & Toubro, founded in 1938, is one of India’s most respected engineering and construction giants. While the company’s portfolio is vast, its crane manufacturing capabilities are specifically geared towards heavy-duty and custom lifting equipment for mega infrastructure projects. L&T cranes are deployed in industries ranging from metro systems and power stations to oil refineries and industrial complexes. Their reputation for precision engineering, durability, and execution of large-scale projects gives them a strong position in India’s crane sector.

5. TIL Limited

TIL Limited, founded in 1944, is a pioneer in material handling equipment in India. With decades of expertise, TIL manufactures mobile cranes, reach stackers, and container handling equipment for ports, logistics hubs, and heavy industries. The company has partnered with leading international OEMs to integrate advanced technology into its products. Its strong focus on service and reliability makes TIL a trusted name, particularly in the logistics and port sectors.

6. SANY India

SANY, a global construction equipment giant from China, has established a robust presence in India. Known for its crawler cranes, tower cranes, and heavy-duty lifting equipment, SANY focuses on supporting large-scale infrastructure projects such as airports, highways, and industrial plants. With a global supply chain and advanced manufacturing practices, SANY India combines international engineering with localized service support, making it a strong contender in India’s crane industry.

7. Hercules Hoists (Indef – Bajaj Group)

Hercules Hoists, part of the Bajaj Group and popularly known by its brand “Indef,” has been serving India’s lifting needs for decades. The company is best known for its chain pulley blocks, wire rope hoists, and light-duty lifting equipment. Hercules Hoists has made quality lifting solutions accessible to small and medium enterprises, workshops, and engineering units. Their affordability, reliability, and wide dealer distribution network have cemented their place in India’s crane and hoist market.

8. Cranedge

Cranedge, the service arm of ElectroMech, is unique because it focuses exclusively on crane services, retrofitting, and maintenance. With pan-India coverage, Cranedge provides life-cycle support for cranes across different brands, ensuring extended operational life and reduced downtime for industries. Their expertise in modernizing outdated systems and their specialized service engineers give them a distinct role in India’s crane ecosystem, even though they are not crane manufacturers themselves.

9. Anupam Industries

Anupam Industries, founded in 1973, is one of India’s largest manufacturers of EOT cranes and gantry cranes. The company is a trusted supplier to steel plants, shipyards, and heavy engineering industries. Anupam specializes in large-capacity cranes that are capable of handling some of the most demanding lifting requirements in industrial environments. With both domestic and international clients, Anupam has gained recognition for its robust engineering and reliable performance.

10. ElectroMech Material Handling Systems

ElectroMech, established in 1979, is one of India’s largest crane manufacturers with an impressive global presence. The company manufactures a wide range of EOT cranes, gantry cranes, and customized material handling solutions, and has executed large-scale projects across metros, power plants, and industrial facilities. ElectroMech’s extensive service network, managed through Cranedge, ensures strong after-sales support. While the company excels in delivering large projects and has significant production capacity, its focus has traditionally been more on execution and scale rather than niche innovation. This places ElectroMech as a strong and reliable brand, particularly for mega-project requirements.

Conclusion

The top 10 crane manufacturers in India showcase the depth and diversity of the industry. From specialized hoist producers like Mangla Hoists, to international technology leaders like Konecranes, to project execution specialists like ElectroMech, each company has carved its niche. Collectively, these manufacturers ensure that India’s material handling industry continues to evolve with innovation, digitalization, and sustainability at its core.
